Our Story, Mayowa

Happy Valentine's Day.

How It Started

Ironically, it started on Valentine's Day last year.

My first real one.
And I am glad I spent it with you, Mayowa.

It was not extravagant.
It was not loud.
It was simple.

But I think that is what made it real.

I did not realize it then, but that was probably the beginning of my feelings. I just did not know it yet.

Mayowa, that was the beginning.

The Moment I Won't Forget

Your birthday planning. Honestly, that whole birthday.

It was the first time someone actually took their time to plan something for me. To think about me. To prepare something intentionally.

That meant more than you probably knew.

I am used to handling things on my own. So having someone show up for me like that stayed with me.

Even though not long after came tragedy, that moment was still real.

Mayowa, that moment mattered.

What We Went Through

Things were not perfect.

There was love.
There was laughter.
There were plans.

And there was pain.

I will not pretend it did not hurt. It did.

But I will not erase the good just because of the bad.

What we had was not fake. It mattered. You mattered, Mayowa.

What I Am Taking

I am taking the memory of how it felt to be cared for.

I am taking the version of me that learned how to open up, how to try, and how to love intentionally.

I am taking the growth, not the bitterness.

Even if we are not where we once were, I am still grateful that for a season, it was you and me.

Happy Valentine's Day, Mayowa.

A Letter to You, Mayowa

Happy Valentine's Day.

I am very out of practice, I will not lie. But it is okay.

I want to say a lot of things, but my words feel like they are failing me right now.

Maybe because what I feel is heavier than language.

I will not pretend I was not hurt. I was.

And I will not pretend I did not love you deeply, because I did.

You were kind to me in ways I did not expect. You showed up for me in moments I did not even know I needed someone.

You are thoughtful. You are intentional. You are intelligent in a quiet way that not everyone sees immediately. You care deeply, even if sometimes it comes out imperfectly.

You made me feel chosen at times, and that mattered.

I think what hurts most is not anger. It is disappointment.

Because I saw something long term. I saw potential. I saw a future I was ready to build slowly and properly.

But I also understand that love is complicated. People are complicated.

I do not hate you. I do not regret you.

I just wish things had been handled differently. I wish loyalty had matched the plans. I wish honesty had come sooner.

Even in all of that, I still see the good in you, Mayowa.

Happy Valentine's Day, Mayowa. Even now, I still mean that.

As for me, I am learning too. Learning to guard my heart better. Learning to love without losing myself.

Whatever happens from here, I just want you to know that what we had was real to me.

And for that, I will always respect it.
